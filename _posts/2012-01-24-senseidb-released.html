What is it?

One-liner:

SenseiDB is a distributed data system optimized for scenarios with rapid updates and complex queries, including full-text search capabilities.

Sensei is used in many places to power LinkedIn.com, powering search pages to analytics products. Most notably the LinkedIn Homepage.

What is it NOT?

To clear up some confusions from some comments on Hacker News, Sensei is not meant to replace every features of a RDBMS. As with any systems, design trade-offs were made for the primary problem the system tries to solve:

  • Sensei is NOT relational - JOIN operations are not supported. We adopt the philosophy of NOSQL data systems of data to be de-normalized.
  • Sensei is NOT transactional - although we provide durability and eventual consistency guarantees, we don't support a full transactional insert model, e.g. roll-back etc.
  • Eventual Consistency - Sensei does NOT have the same consistency guarantees that a traditional RDBMS supports. Instead, we guarantee the data to be eventual consistent, e.g. at All nodes will guarantee to converge to a consistent state.
